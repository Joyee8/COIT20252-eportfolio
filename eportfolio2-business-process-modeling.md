## Artefact 1 – IBM: What is Business Process Modeling and Notation (BPMN)?

[![IBM BPMN Article](https://img.youtube.com/vi/-IRN9yQYE6o/mqdefault.jpg)](https://www.ibm.com/think/topics/bpmn)

**Artefact:** IBM 2025, *What is Business Process Modeling and Notation (BPMN)?*, IBM Think, viewed April 2025, <https://www.ibm.com/think/topics/bpmn>

This artefact is an online article published by IBM that explains BPMN as the global standard for modelling business processes. It describes how BPMN provides a graphical notation for representing workflows using standardised symbols including events, activities, gateways, and sequence flows. The article explains that BPMN enables business analysts, technical developers, and business managers to share a common understanding of how processes are designed and executed, bridging the communication gap between business and IT teams (IBM 2025, p. 1).

I chose this artefact because it introduced me to BPMN from the perspective of a leading global technology company, which helped me understand how widely it is used in industry beyond the classroom. Before engaging with this resource, I understood that process modelling involved drawing diagrams, but I did not fully appreciate that a globally standardised notation existed for this purpose. Learning that BPMN is maintained by the Object Management Group and recognised as an ISO standard changed my view of its credibility and importance in professional BPM practice. This artefact is meaningful evidence of my learning because it helped me understand that effective business process modelling requires a standardised approach, not just any type of diagram.

## Artefact 3 – Boombirds: The Basics of Business Process Modelling Notation (BPMN)

[![Boombirds BPMN Blog](https://www.boombirds.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/bpmn-basics-header.jpg)](https://www.boombirds.com/blog/2025/03/27/business-process-modelling-notation-bpmn/)

**Artefact:** Kidd, J 2025, *The Basics of Business Process Modelling Notation (BPMN)*, Boombirds, 27 March, viewed April 2025, <https://www.boombirds.com/blog/2025/03/27/business-process-modelling-notation-bpmn/>

This artefact is a blog post published in March 2025 by Jonathan Kidd that introduces the core elements of BPMN, explaining that it is a graphical notation used to define and communicate business processes that can then be translated into BPM software. The post describes how BPMN 2.0 uses swim lanes, message flows, and data object symbols to show how work is performed across departments and systems, making it easier for all stakeholders to identify what needs improvement (Kidd 2025, p. 1).

I chose this artefact because it was published in 2025 and provided a beginner-friendly explanation of BPMN elements that I could directly relate to the modelling concepts introduced in this unit. The explanation of swim lanes was particularly helpful because it showed me how BPMN visually represents responsibilities across different departments, which I had not fully grasped from reading text descriptions alone. This improved my understanding of BPM by showing how process models visually capture accountability, workflow direction, and cross-departmental handoffs. I found this artefact meaningful because it bridged my theoretical knowledge of BPMN with a practical understanding of how notation elements function together in a real business process diagram.
